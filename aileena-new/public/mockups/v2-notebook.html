In the racks that train the models you talk to every day, there's a small black ring buried between the optical module and the switch. It costs less than a coffee. Almost nobody knows its name.

But if it warps, the laser inside the module doesn't aim right anymore. The light scatters. The bytes don't land. The training run pauses.

it's small. but the whole machine gets upset when it breaks.

That's why three suppliers in the world get to make it. Why a single missed shipment from one of them shows up on Nvidia's earnings call. And why nobody on the public-relations team has ever said its name out loud.
