Five PCBs sit inside a current-generation AI rack — HDI compute, switch, mid plane, orthogonal backplane, and the substrate-like board that decides 2027.

↑ each numbered in the diagram. start with ①.

Taiwan held the high-layer-board market for two decades. Mainland China overtook them on fifth-order 22-layer HDI in 2025 — Victory Giant at ~90% yield against Unimicron at ~80%. The difference is factory age: 2024-greenfield versus a 2001 line.

The point isn't who makes the board today. It's whose factory is young enough to make the next one. ↑ this is the whole investing thesis

Why the mid plane exists at all: the NVIDIA 576 rack replaces 5,000+ copper cables with a 44-layer orthogonal stack — M9 + Q-cloth + fourth-gen copper foil, 18 per cabinet. Mechanical drilling retires. Laser drilling, mostly Han's Laser, takes >60% domestic share.

remember: M9 + Q-cloth retires the old drill. that's the third-order effect.

Three barriers reshuffling share, in order: substrate-like PCB at 10 µm lines (Avary has Apple SLP DNA, Victory Giant is committing capex); M9 / Q-cloth opening Han's Laser; copper sintering for the mid plane + backplane — industry-first and execution-speed-led.

Plus the parallel Google TPU build (forecast revised 2.5–3M → 4M chips at ~$9k each; uses 22–24 layer multi-high-layer, not HDI; ISU / Wus / ChaoYing incumbent; Victory Giant queuing for ~6 month certification).
